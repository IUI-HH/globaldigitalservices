Basics and History of AI

Early Foundations

Learn the Early Foundations of AI

The concept of AI dates back to 400 BCE when philosophers would contemplate the term “automaton,” meaning “acting of one’s own will.” This includes reacting to a patron’s comments, coming up with solutions when prompted, and in some cases, problem solving. Learn more at Tableau.

Initial Experiments

Learn About Early Trials

It wasn’t until the 1920s that the world began experimenting with the concept of an artificial brain, setting the stage for modern developments. Learn more at IBM.

Pioneers of AI

Meet AI's Makers

By the 1950’s, familiar names such as Alan Turing, publisher of the Computer Machinery and Intelligence test, and Arthur Samuel, developer of the Checkers program which allows a one player option, began to appear. Names such as these two men were just the beginning of the AI boom.

Modern Innovations

Learn how AI has Changed

Eventually names such as John McCarthy (LISP), The American Association of Artificial Intelligence (AAAI), Cynthia Breazeal (first robot to simulate human emotion), and SIRI (Apple co.), would further push the AI industry into our everyday lives.

AI Today

AI from a Modern Perspective

As of 2015, OpenAI, a San Francisco startup, created a program known as ChatGPT. This program, which had the ability to have a conversation or write whatever it was prompted to write, would soon be seen as a plagiarism-like act that many educators would then blame all of AI for. However, the term “AI” is not only a lot larger than ChatGPT, but also includes other pros and cons in the education environment.
