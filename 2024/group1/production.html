Post-Production

Another relatively non-controversial area in which generative AI is being used in filmmaking is in post-production, to add lighting effects, adjust colors, and automate tasks involved in video editing and dubbing.

Uses

Software like Adobe Firefly and Topaz Labs allows post-production crew members to quickly and efficiently enhance film clips by cleaning up backgrounds, color grading, and overlaying complex lighting effects. Generative AI for visual effects are also being used for film restoration. Ridley

Scott's seminal sci-fi noir "Blade Runner" received a restoration and re-release in 2007, known as "Blade Runner: The Final Cut." The restoration process involved the use of deep learning algorithms to enhance the film's visual quality and remove imperfections, such as scratches and dirt, from the original footage. AI-based restoration techniques have become increasingly prevalent in film preservation efforts, allowing classic movies to be remastered for modern audiences while preserving their original aesthetic.

Beyond visual effects, Neutron provides similar benefits for audio mixing and noise reduction. Flawless AI can be used to automate the process of matching actor mouths when dubbing scenes into other languages. As in other areas of production, these tools can reduce production times and costs, ultimately, making sophisticated filmmaking techniques available to a wider set of users.

Concerns

As in all areas of filmmaking, the increasing use of generative AI brings with it fears for job security, ethical considerations related to copyright protection for AI-enhanced work, and skepticism that the work created with AI will not be perceived to be as beautiful or natural as work created by humans.
