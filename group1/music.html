Music

Audio production can benefit from the use of generative AI tools for providing inspiration and improving collaboration, enhancing recordings, generating music using virtual music ensembles, and creating sound designs for immersive soundscapes.

Uses

Tools like AIVA, Mubert, and Audiomodern can be used to identify and isolate song stems (melody, rhythm, etc.) for faster music production and to create music to complement various moods. As in other production areas, these tools allow crew members to automate some time-consuming tasks, making the process more efficient and giving them more time to devote to other work.

In addition, AI-generated music can be modified in real-time without the need to re-record musicians. Ultimately, these benefits can lead to reductions in production times and costs associated with music for film.

Concerns

The primary area of concern for the use of AI-generated music in film is that there is no need to obtain approval for the use of copyrighted material used to train the AIs.

This can be seen as a pro for filmmakers in terms of savings in costs and time, but as a con for musicians and composers, who not only lose money but lose the right to have a say in how their own work is used.
